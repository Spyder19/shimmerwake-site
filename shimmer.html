The Shimmer

The artifact vibrates, and a shimmering blue pulse travels across the water—like moonlight refracted through a crystal, soft, haunting, impossible to process.
1349 Champ slips through first, her bulk distorting the glow.
1350 The Dauntless glides in—not so much sailing as being drawn. Hull and crew ghost into the portal, vanishing before anyone can capture it. There's no sound. Only mist.
1352 A teenager captures the portal flicker on a cracked phone. The shimmer's already gone.
“The best departures make less noise than a yawn, trail fewer ripples than a lie, and leave behind only the shimmering possibility that maybe, just maybe, something wonderful passed this way.”
1443 Vanessa: "Tonight the world bent further than I expected."
1444 Played a binaural focus track over the boat PA—just to focus—but the crystal began to hum: bright, warm, weirdly harmonic.
1445 Mist hovered. The portal shimmered like a held breath.
1446 Champ responded—not with words, but intention. The feeling hit us like thunder behind the eyes: Help... Understand...
“It shimmered because it was real. It shimmered because maybe we were meant to feel it—not record it. Just remember it.”
1434 The crystal flared. The song echoed. The Dauntless went through.
1436 Adamsian Reflection: “Sometimes the universe gives you what you need—a relic, a tone, a thick-headed crew—if you're just odd enough to take the leap.”
